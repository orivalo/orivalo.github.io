How Popular is E-Commerce?

Survey Findings (20 Participants)

  • b. Have shopped but not purchased online: 2 people (10%)
  • c. Frequency of purchases: Once a year: 8 | Once a month: 4 | Once a week: 2 | Varies: 4
  • d. Age range: 18–25: 13 | 26–39: 4 | 40–50: 2 | Over 50: 1
  • e. Gender: Men: 10 | Women: 10
  • f. Level of education: High School: 4 | Some College: 3 | College Graduate: 7 | Graduate School: 6
  • g. Favorite online shopping sites: Amazon (4), Trendyol (3), Shein (3), PlayStation Store (2), Wildberries (2).

Conclusions & Analysis

The survey results clearly demonstrate that e-commerce is deeply integrated into daily life, with 90% of respondents having completed an online purchase. The demographic data indicates a particularly strong inclination toward online shopping among younger adults (18-25 age range) and individuals with a college degree or higher.

These local findings accurately reflect broader industry trends. According to reports from the Pew Internet and American Life Project, a vast majority of internet users have purchased goods online, establishing e-commerce as a societal norm. Furthermore, data from eMarketer and the E-Commerce Times consistently highlights the dominance of large-scale retail platforms like Amazon, alongside the rising global popularity of fast-fashion websites such as Shein. This aligns perfectly with the favorite sites selected by our respondents. Finally, market insights from ClickZ suggest that mobile accessibility continues to drive higher shopping frequencies, which is evident in the portion of our respondents who shop on a monthly or weekly basis.
